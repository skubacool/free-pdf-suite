        The Same Tools, Without the Upload

        iLovePDF is a capable suite, but it processes your files on its servers: every document you merge, compress or sign is uploaded, and the free tier adds task limits, sign-up prompts and occasional waits. For most everyday PDF jobs none of that is necessary — the work can run directly in your browser. upmypdf does exactly that, covering the same core tasks (merge, split, compress, rotate, convert to and from Office and images, page numbers, watermarks and more) with no file ever leaving your machine.

        That difference matters most for sensitive documents — contracts, statements, IDs — which you may not want sitting on a third-party server even briefly. It also means no per-day caps, no account, and no watermark stamped on your output. Everything here is free and unlimited.

        Why In-Browser Processing Wins

        Because the tools are plain JavaScript running locally, the only thing downloaded is the code itself — once, and cached. Your documents are read, edited and saved on your device. There is no upload step to wait for, nothing to delete from a server afterwards, and nothing that could be retained or indexed.
